Case Study: ABC Jewelry Consulting

Background

This was contract work for an upcoming jewelry consulting company. Their core mission of this company is to partner with small to midsize jewelry brands/designers to promote and sell their products for commission.



Problem:

From this point on, we’ll refer to the jewelry consulting company as ABC Jewels. ABC Jewels is aiming to disrupt the jewelry industry by partnering directly with small to midsize brands and selling their jewelry around the world. ABC Jewels is composed of many jewelry sales consultants with a number of years of experience. Each consultant also has many loyal clients. This software is to help everyone in this B2B2C relationship.

"Basically we augment their sales teams. To do that, we need to manage the products of many brands. We need to find things fast. We need to stay organized.”
- Founding Partner

To manage this global sales team, ABC Jewels needed a product inventory solution that allows their consultants to quickly find jewelry based on specific tastes of their clients. A bonus goal would be that this solution would also help consultants do personalized outreach.



Team Building:

As mentioned before, this was contract work, so it was a side project from my day job. Because ABC Jewelry is Hong Kong based, that meant that I woulc be able to work on it in the evenings after my day job. Of course, I am not a machine, so I was going to need help with guiding the design process. I used the initial upfront fee to budget for a UX lead and designers.

I put a job posting on Upwork and interviewed a handful of people, but I eventually decided to go with Chui Yin Wong. Her experience with UX as well as her understanding of the buying habits of consumers in Asia was invaluble. She led the way in developing our UX approach and guided many of our UX sessions with the client.

Based on my initial discovery, I knew that this project would require a tech stack that allowed for fast prototyping and easy updates to support ABC Jewelry's experimental business models. I won't go into the technical details here, but this helped me narrow down consulting firm company that also had amazing designers, but also a development team that understood the goal for an easier handoff.

User Research:

The target jewelry designers that ABC Jewels works with are based in Hong Kong, one of the most active cities in the international jewelry trade. I started my research by going to Hong Kong and visiting two of the biggest jewelry shows in Asia; The Jewelry and Gem Hong Kong and The Hong Kong International Jewelry Show.

Here I had a chance to talk to many jewelry designers (There were 500+ for each show) as well as talk with ABC Jewels about their goals, history, and motivations. From this, I created very representative personas of all participants in this concept.

The two personas I will address in this case-study are the ABC Jewels Admin and the Jewelry Consultant. There is buyer persona but that applies to the online shop portion of the application. This case study is about the admin side.



Persona #1: Consultant

The Consultant will browse the inventory and share items of interest with the client. They are the front line of ABC Jewels. Their clients are high touch with high expectations. They often need to find unique, inspirations quickly.



Persona #2: Admin

The Jewelry Admin's jobs is to be constantly be adding to inventory as well as supporitng consultant's requests. They will have a large network of sources for stones and jewelry. Searching through products efficiently is paramount.



Domain Research:

At the time, I did not have a lot of exposure to the jewelry industry, especially at these price points ($100K+) so I had my lead designer do a significant amount of domain research to get us up to speed, and we analyzed the findings to create a solid competitor analysis.

At this point, we had a good understanding of our users goals, their pain points, and the domain that we’d be working in. We were confident that we could get started on the UI.



Information Architecture:

To make sure we captured all the required functionality for this project, we created a product requirements document and used that to make the high level information architecture. Throughout this, we met regularly with the stakeholders at ABC Jewelry to ensure we were on the right track.

The complexity of the project grew, but we knew we would trim the fat by the end. Just as I suspected, this would actually be two apps that communicate with each other via an REST API. As we were desigining, the dynamics of ABC's operations were changing due to them being a fast moving startup. Our flexible, agile approach allowed us to keep up.



Design:

After several weeks of late night (At least late for me) design sessions, we finally completed the UI deisgn.

Technical architectural decisions I made in this process ended up saving ABC Jewels a lot of money from the original development estimate. We also shipped earlier than expected due to cutting non-essential features and focusing just on what was needed right now.

Key Outcomes

  • Project finished on schedule
  • Saved client 60% of the original development costs
  • Application is used daily by client
